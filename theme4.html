Public-Private Partnerships

Aligning Public Value and Private Investment in Health Innovation

“Philanthropy won’t scale precision medicine. Partnership will.”

Innovation in healthcare cannot rely on siloed investment or fragmented responsibility. In Europe, cross-sectoral partnerships supported by EU funding—including ECHoS, SPARC, and BRECISE—are demonstrating how public–private alignment can scale infrastructure, digital tools, and genomic platforms.

This session examines what makes collaboration sustainable. It will highlight governance structures that ensure transparency, funding models that reward outcomes, and capital flows that de-risk early-stage technologies. Real examples from GenoMed4All, Joint Cancer Personalized Medicine, and other consortia will illustrate what scalable, accountable innovation looks like in practice.

It also touches on how to incentivize EU-based trials, foster industry alignment with Mission on Cancer goals, and embed patient-centricity into co-designed solutions.

Conclusion:

The next wave of transformation will be built not on competition—but on collective accountability and aligned incentives.

In a world that’s increasingly interconnected and diverse, EAPM is dedicated to spearheading a new era in healthcare — one where precision and personalization redefine the standards of prevention and treatment strategies. Join us in exploring the forefront of medical innovation, where our commitment to individualized care is not just a goal but a revolution.
