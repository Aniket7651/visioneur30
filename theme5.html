Precision Health

Neurology, Cardiology, and Chronic Disease in the AI Era

“The tools exist. The integration does not.”

While oncology leads the narrative on personalized medicine, the opportunity to embed AI, digital biomarkers, and genomics in other disease areas is massive—but underexploited. Cardiology, neurology, and obesity-related disease are ripe for disruption, yet face systemic bottlenecks: siloed care, fragmented data, and misaligned reimbursement.

This session features four thematic spotlights:

  • Cardiology: AI for diagnostics, biomarker-guided pathways, and pan-EU procurement strategies
  • Neurology: Neuro-AI applications, voice biomarkers, and wearable integration in early detection
  • Obesity & Chronic Disease: Precision trials, microbiome-guided interventions, and equity in prevention
  • Cardiometabolic–Cancer Convergence: Predictive analytics for multimorbidity management

Drawing on EAPM’s cross-disease policy recommendations and EHDS-linked projects, these spotlights will present how Europe can replicate oncology’s momentum in other specialties—while avoiding its delays.

Conclusion:

Precision health must become the rule across disease areas—not the exception reserved for cancer.

In a world that’s increasingly interconnected and diverse, EAPM is dedicated to spearheading a new era in healthcare — one where precision and personalization redefine the standards of prevention and treatment strategies. Join us in exploring the forefront of medical innovation, where our commitment to individualized care is not just a goal but a revolution.
